    BASIC HEALTH UNITS OF SAMAMBAIA

    About

    The Basic Health Units (UBS), formerly known as Health Centers, Health Posts, or Family Clinics, are the user's preferred entry point to the Unified Health System (SUS). This is where the care of the population's health begins.

    More information at saude.df.gov.br

    Hours and Location

    Check the operating hours and location of your reference UBS.

    Each UBS is responsible for the health care of a defined population. To find your reference unit, defined by your home address, click here.

    Priority of Service

    The priority of service is determined through Risk Classification, a process that assesses the severity of the situation using clinical and vulnerability criteria.

    Required Documents

    • Official photo ID;
    • National Health Card (SUS Card);
    • It is recommended to bring proof of residence (not mandatory);
    • Documentation is not required for homeless people.

    Services available to the population:

    • Vaccination room, as provided by the National Immunization Program;
    • Pharmacy dispensing medications for Primary Care, open until 10:00 PM;
    • Dispensing of psychotropic drugs, according to pharmaceutical assistance regulations;
    • Laboratory test collection room and electrocardiogram.
